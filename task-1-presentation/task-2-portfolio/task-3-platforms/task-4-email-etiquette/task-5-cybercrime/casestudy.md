Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most rapidly growing forms of cybercrime in India, particularly targeting college students and young adults who frequently use apps like GPay, PhonePe, and Paytm.
In a typical UPI fraud scenario, a victim receives a call or message from someone posing as a bank representative or customer care agent. The fraudster informs the victim that their account has been compromised or that they have won a prize. They then ask the victim to share their UPI PIN, OTP, or to scan a QR code to "verify" their account. As soon as the victim complies, money is instantly debited from their account.
In one realistic scenario, a first-year college student received a WhatsApp message claiming he had won a scholarship of ₹10,000. The message asked him to scan a QR code to "receive" the money. Unaware that scanning a QR code on UPI sends money rather than receives it, he scanned it and lost ₹8,000 from his account within seconds.
UPI fraud primarily targets students, senior citizens, and first-time digital payment users. The consequences include immediate financial loss, emotional distress, and in some cases, identity theft if personal documents were shared during the interaction.
The key lesson is: you never need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to receive money. Any such request is always a fraud.

Source: National Cyber Crime Portal — cybercrime.gov.in
